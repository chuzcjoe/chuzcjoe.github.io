# 1. Overview

An arbitrary function can be approximated by a sum of `sines` and `cosines` of increasing frequencies. These `sines` and `cosines` basically forms orthogonal basis for the space of possible functions, just like in 2D space, we have x and y coordinates that form basis that could represent any 2D vectors in the space.
